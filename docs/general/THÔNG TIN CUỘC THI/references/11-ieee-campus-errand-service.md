# [11] A Cross-platform Errand Service Application for Campus

**Source:** IEEE Xplore
**URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9930264/
**Published:** 2022, IEEE 13th International Conference on Software Engineering and Service Science (ICSESS), pp. 155–160

## Paper Details

**Title:** "A Cross-platform Errand Service Application for Campus with a Dual-user Identity Model"

**Authors:** Chenliang Tian, Xinyi Fang, Xu Yang, Yapeng Wang
(Faculty of Applied Science, Macao Polytechnic University, Macao SAR, China)

## Abstract

Addresses service gaps created when off-campus personnel cannot access university grounds during epidemics. Developed **AoAoRun** — "an errand services application for university students and faculties on campus using a dual-user identity model," allowing both groups to serve as providers and recipients.

## Key Features

- Blockchain for data backup
- Credit scoring mechanism (prioritizes orders from higher-rated users)
- Dual-identity model: flexible role-switching among campus members
- Cross-platform implementation

## Key Findings

- **53 volunteers** participated in user testing
- Application "greatly improves the utilization of resources and increases the efficiency of students' study and life"
- Integration of blockchain + credit systems differentiates from existing errand services

## Relevance to Viecz

AoAoRun is a close academic reference for campus-focused micro-task platforms. Viecz's approach is similar (dual-user model, campus focus) but adds escrow payments, real-time chat, and geolocation — features not present in AoAoRun.
