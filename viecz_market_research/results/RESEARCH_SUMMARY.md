## Market Gaps and Opportunities

### Identified Gaps

1. **No dedicated campus errand platforms in SE Asia** (except potentially dormant ones)
   - Vietnam: Completely unserved
   - Indonesia: Unserved (Grab/Gojek generic)
   - Thailand: Unserved (dominated by food delivery)
   - Philippines: Unserved (remote gig economy dominant)
   - Malaysia: Partially addressed by GoGetter (non-campus-specific)

2. **Hyperlocal trust and verification challenges**
   - Connect Sphere .edu email model shows promise
   - Campus verification more reliable than general location verification

3. **Market thickness on college campuses**
   - Zero-commission Connect Sphere model addresses but is unproven at scale
   - AoAoRun's blockchain credit system approach

### Opportunities for Viecz

1. **Campus-First Strategy**: All identified working platforms either focus on general gig work or came much later to campuses
2. **Hyperlocal Advantage**: University boundaries create natural trust boundaries
3. **Vertical Integration**: Bundle multiple micro-service types (delivery, task services, peer selling)
4. **Regional Expansion Path**: Start Vietnam → expand to Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines
5. **Regulatory Navigation**: Learn from GoGetter's EPF integration and Singapore's Platform Workers Act
