## Key Research Findings

### Market Overview
- **11 competitor platforms** identified (active, dead, or pivoted)
- **10 academic publications** validating market demand
- **Southeast Asia gig economy:** USD 1.61B+ (Malaysia alone), 23% YoY growth
- **Student participation:** 15% of gig workers are part-time students

### Southeast Asia Coverage
| Country | Dedicated Campus Platforms | Regional Players | Status |
|---------|---------------------------|-----------------|--------|
| Vietnam | None found | Grab | Market gap |
| Indonesia | None found | Grab/Gojek (merged) | Market gap |
| Thailand | None found | Line Man Wongnai | Market gap |
| Philippines | None found | Upwork/Fiverr | Remote-only |
| Malaysia | GoGetter (partial) | GoGetter | Partially served |
| Singapore | NUS internal scheme | iTask, Grab | Regulated |

### Market Gaps
1. **No dedicated campus errand platforms in Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines**
2. **Hyperlocal verification challenges** - Connect Sphere's .edu email model shows promise
3. **Market thickness on campuses** - Zero-commission approaches being tested
4. **Regulatory frameworks** - Emerging opportunity before legislation

### Academic Validation
- **AoAoRun (IEEE 2022):** Dual-user model with blockchain credit system - 53 user study showed efficiency gains
- **Connect Sphere (2025):** Two peer-reviewed publications on zero-commission hyperlocal P2P - direct validation of campus marketplace demand
- **Malaysian Youth Study (2025):** 385 respondents show 19-30 age group strong participation, key motivation = flexible hours + autonomy
- **Thai University Study (2024):** 27.14% online delivery penetration among students

### Regulatory Landscape
- **Singapore:** Platform Workers Act (Jan 2025) - social security cost implications
- **Malaysia:** GoGetter already integrating EPF, regulatory cooperation model emerging
- **Vietnam/Indonesia/Thailand/Philippines:** Limited specific gig economy regulation identified - first-mover advantage opportunity

## Questions and Notes

### Why these 11 platforms?
These represent:
- **Direct campus competitors:** AoAoRun, Connect Sphere, Errandly, Campus Sphere
- **Regional gig players with student presence:** GoGetter, iTask, Grab, Gojek
- **Global reference models:** Lugg (specialized service), Upwork/Fiverr (remote)
- **Failed platform for learning:** Timesaverz (what not to do)

### What about China's campus errand market?
Covered via AoAoRun (Macau). Chinese domestic platforms not extensively researched (beyond Macau academic publication) due to language/access barriers. Food delivery dominance in China (Meituan, Ele.me) noted in research.

### Why focus on Southeast Asia?
Viecz operations are Vietnam-based. Research expanded to cover Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Philippines, Indonesia for:
1. Regional expansion potential
2. Regulatory trend analysis
3. Operational models (especially GoGetter Malaysia)
4. Market maturity comparison

### Are these all the platforms?
This represents all publicly documented, searchable platforms. "Dark" platforms or very new Vietnamese startups may exist but lacked online presence/documentation during research (March 2026).
