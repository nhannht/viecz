## Executive Summary

Comprehensive research on Vietnamese student economic behavior has been conducted using credible sources from government agencies (GSO, MOLISA), academic institutions, World Bank analyses, and industry reports. The data reveals Vietnamese university students face significant financial pressure, with average monthly expenses ranging from 2-7 million VND nationally, and approximately 5+ million VND in TP.HCM. Part-time work participation is a key financial coping mechanism, with about 22% of students engaging in paid work, typically at hourly rates of 20,000-60,000 VND/hour. Digital payment adoption among younger demographics is strong, with e-wallets dominating (62% use MoMo) and bank transfers being preferred for larger transactions.

## Key Findings by Category

### Student Spending and Living Costs
- **National average monthly expense**: 2-7 million VND/month (varies by accommodation)
- **TP.HCM average**: 5 million VND/month (excluding tuition)
- **Annual education spending**: 9.5 million VND/year (2024, up 36.3% from 2022)
- **Urban-rural gap**: Urban students' costs are 2.1x higher than rural (10.7M vs 5.1M VND/year)
- **Accommodation patterns**: 52.34% live with family, 40.43% in rental housing, 7.23% in dormitories

### Part-Time Work & Income
- **Participation rate**: 22.1% of university/vocational students work part-time (GSO 2018)
- **Work intensity**: 30.5% work <5 hours/week; balance between study and work highly variable
- **Hourly wages**: Market range 20,000-60,000 VND/hour; common rate 15,000-20,000 VND/hour
- **Legal minimum (2026)**: Zone I (TP.HCM) 25,500 VND/hour
- **Monthly income from PT work**: 1-2 million VND (typical 30 hours/week)
- **Top motivations**: Professional skills development (80.8%) and income needs (80.8%)
- **Job types**: Retail, food service, tutoring, delivery; simple jobs requiring few specialized skills
- **Job search method**: 38% find jobs via Facebook

### Informal and Gig Work
- **Youth informal employment rate**: 79.5% of young workers (ILO 2015)
- **Out-of-school youth (15-17)**: 18.3% working instead of studying
- **Vulnerable employment**: 34.6% in precarious work (own-account, unpaid family work)
- **Gig economy participation**: Growing via Grab, Be, ShopeeFood, Vlance; 70% can negotiate rates
- **NEET youth (2025)**: 1.4 million aged 15-24 (10.2% of youth population)

### Digital Payment Adoption
- **E-wallet overall adoption**: ~60% of population; 60%+ use multiple wallets
- **MoMo dominance**: 62% of users (most popular in 2024); 31M users, 140K merchant points
- **Usage frequency**: 66% use e-wallets weekly; 35% use 3-5 times/week; 30% daily
- **Young adults (under 30)**: 37.95% use e-banking; 30.12% use e-payments
- **Bank transfers**: Most widely adopted for security; preferred for larger sums
- **Online transaction value**: USD 18.2B in 2024; 73% via mobile wallet super-apps
- **ZaloPay integration**: Growing due to Zalo messaging integration and education fee solutions
- **BNPL adoption**: Growing appeal for Gen Z without credit cards

### Financial Literacy & Behavior
- **Emergency funds**: 72.8% maintain emergency savings
- **Budgeting**: 70.6% have a budget
- **Spending control**: Only 54.8% avoid overdrafts; 52.2-47.8% overspend monthly income
- **Gen Z financial literacy scores**: Overall 66.8 (age 20-29), long-term planning 23.8 (weak)
- **Knowledge gaps**: 67% report confusion about managing finances; ~70% never learned finance in school
- **Investment participation**: Only 42% engage in investing (risk-averse perception)

### Spending Sufficiency Assessment
- **Just sufficient**: 64.7% of students
- **Difficulty**: 23.4% report financial difficulty
- **No budget pressure**: 11.9% never spend all their monthly allowance

### Gender and Equity Issues
- **University access inequality**: 80% of top income quintile attend university vs. 10% of bottom two quintiles
- **Tuition cost burden**: 46% of public sector costs, 69% of private sector costs
